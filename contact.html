Formerly a West Point cadet and prospective Army officer, I am now pursuing a career in computer programming. Dismissal for academic shortcomings during my senior year has forced me to choose an alternate path, now that I am no longer in the U.S. Army. I am concurrently working as a low-voltage technician while enrolled in Georgia Tech's coding bootcamp.


Though my career in the Profession of Arms has ended prematurely, I am still determined to take part in the defense of my country from outside attack. The increasing use of weaponized disinformation, commercial drones, and direct incursion of computer networks by America's strategic rivals has marked the maturation of cyberspace as a relevant domain of large-scale conflict. As I prepare to complete my education and advance my expertise as a programmer, I intend to pursue a career in information security with a focus on the theory and practice of cyber warfare.
