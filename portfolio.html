After growing up in Savannah and graduating high school in 2012, I completed four years at West Point before being dismissed for academic deficiency. According to West Point's Academic Board, the two class failures I incurred during my senior year constitute a violation of contract, and I now owe three years' service in the U.S. Army as recoupment for the money spent on my terminated education. So as to avoid a complete waste of my talents and taxpayer dollars, I am attempting to re-purpose my background in mathematics and systems engineering by reenlisting as a 17C (Cyber Operations Specialist).

After waiting ten months to be properly discharged, I enrolled in this course in order to continue learning without committing to the completion of my bachelor's degree. Though I'm not sure when I'll be able to continue my college educaiton, I intend to study Computer Science and become a software developer once I have resolved my debt to the federal government.
